## 1. The Alpha Trader Equation

```
Alpha Trader = rational + intelligent + skilled + conscientious + calibrated confidence
```

- **Rationality (RQ)** is more important than IQ for trading — high-IQ people suffer MORE from confirmation bias
- **Overconfidence** is the single best predictor of trading failure
- **Conscientiousness** is the #1 Big Five personality trait correlated with income and is trainable (rises with age)
- Risk appetite deliberately excluded from the equation — it can be dialed with rules; traders at every point on the spectrum succeed

### Survival Statistics

| Venue | Success Rate |
|---|---|
| Independent day traders | 3–5% |
| Institutional (bank) traders | ~50% (still employed/profitable at 3 years) |
| Fund managers beating benchmark (1 year) | 28% |
| Fund managers beating benchmark (10 years) | 11% |

Taiwan day trading study (2017): 80% lose in any 6-month window; only 1% show persistent skill.

## 2. Seven-Stage Narrative Cycle

**Critical insight**: Narrative often turns BEFORE price. Turning points where narrative changed but price hasn't = most profitable moments.

| Stage | Description | Trading Implication |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Under the Radar | New narrative forming, few aware | Holy Grail to identify early; risk of boredom before catalyst |
| 2. Momentum Builds | Story goes viral among professionals; reflexivity kicks in | Trend followers enter; price gaps on clarifying news |
| 3. Primary Trend | Drumbeat; pullbacks bought; news reinforces price | Can last surprisingly long; "well-known" ≠ exhausted |
| 4. First Cracks | Bad news threatens; early profits taken; meaningful pullback | Believers buy dip; weaker hands shaken out |
| 5. Final Hype Wave | FOMO; non-experts join; choppy, euphoric | Most volatile; most surprising |
| 6. Peak and Turn | Narrative changes but price may still make new highs | **Best setup**: narrative turned, price hasn't caught up |
| 7. The End | Interest fades; shorts hammer, longs slowly exit | Theme is over; move on |

Operates on multiple time fractals — 24-hour cycles to multi-month arcs.

## 3. Type I / II / III Conviction System

Evolved from Art of Currency Trading's 3/4/5-star system with explicit formulas.

| Type | Description | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| **Type I** (Run of the Mill) | Standard trade, regular process | 1–3% of free capital |
| **Type II** (High Conviction) | Major narrative shift, significant dislocation; 4–5 per year | **3% of free capital + 10% of YTD P&L** |
| **Type III** (Outlier Opportunity) | Watershed moment; can make your year/career | Up to 50% of YTD P&L if in strong position |

- Risk increases **exponentially**, not linearly, with conviction
- Type II formula creates a call-option P&L structure: similar downside, much higher upside
- **If down on year or near stop loss**: even Type III stays small — wait for better positioning

### Monthly Stop Loss Scale (example for $4M stop / $10M target)

| YTD P&L | Monthly Stop |
|---|---|
| < $0 | $200k |
| $0–2M | $400k |
| $2–4M | $600k |
| $4–6M | $800k |
| $6–8M | $1.0M |
| $8–10M | $1.2M |
| > $10M | $1.5M |

## 4. Complete Trade Lifecycle (7 Steps)

### Step 1: Idea Generation (Six Sources)

1. **Surfing the narrative** — #1 source; feel the winds change before others
2. **News and events** — react, don't predict; flat into events = strongest position
3. **The Run-Up Trade** — prices move predictably in days before major events (positioning reduction + obvious trade + lotto ticket)
4. **"Everybody's Bearish, Nobody's Short"** — strong consensus + no positioning = impulsive move once event passes
5. **Deviation from fair value** — cross-market correlation divergence scan
6. **Pattern recognition** — seasonals, analogs, indicators (September seasonal, bubble deflation at ~85%, month-end dollar effect)

### Step 2: Filtering (Five Dimensions + Alternative Hypotheses)

1. Where in the narrative cycle?
2. Does positioning strengthen or weaken the idea?
3. Upcoming events — pros or cons?
4. Do correlated markets support or contradict?
5. Technical setup — good entry, stop, R:R?
6. **Alternative hypotheses**: Pre-mortem, peer feedback, "strong opinions weakly held"

### Step 3: Tactics

- Product selection (liquidity, TC, leverage)
- Entry point (limit orders preferred; dream entry vs. realistic)
- Stop loss (≥1 ADR away)
- **Reassessment triggers** (record ex-ante): stop, TP, time trigger, level-to-tighten, new info trigger, cross-market trigger

### Step 4: Position Sizing

Six factors: Conviction (I/II/III), free capital, YTD P&L, market vol, entry-to-stop distance, gap/liquidity risk.

```
Position size = $ at risk / (entry - stop loss)
```

Position size is the OUTPUT, not the input.

### Step 5: Execution

- Limit orders > market orders for TC savings
- Hybrid human + algo for large trades
- Futures often tighter than ETFs
- Stop below round numbers (sell), above round numbers (buy)

### Step 6: Tracking

- Default action = do nothing
- Deviate only on reassessment trigger or unambiguous new information
- "Assume new information is mostly noise"

### Step 7: Performance Analysis

- Monthly journal review (not after each trade — too emotional)
- What is working? What is not?
- Stop thinking about past trades; think forward

## 5. Analysis of Competing Hypotheses (ACH)

From CIA's Heuer (*Psychology of Intelligence Analysis*):

1. Identify possible hypotheses (diverse perspectives)
2. List evidence for/against each
3. Build matrix: hypotheses × evidence
4. Assess diagnosticity — which evidence items distinguish between hypotheses
5. Refine: delete non-diagnostic evidence
6. Draw conclusions by trying to DISPROVE (not prove)
7. Sensitivity analysis: what if key evidence is wrong?
8. Report relative likelihood of ALL hypotheses
9. Set milestones/tripwires for future observation

**Key**: Explore alternative hypotheses BEFORE entering a trade.

## 6. Behavioral Finance — Extended Framework

### New Biases (beyond Art of Currency Trading)

| Bias | Mechanism | Countermeasure |
|---|---|---|
| **Grizzly bias** | Bears sound smarter; negativity premium; permabears famous despite being wrong | SPX positive 73% of years; don't be permabear or permabull |
| **Squirrel-chasing** | Entering trades because price is moving — "somebody must know something!" | 97% of price action is noise; could be divorce liquidation or tax loss selling |
| **Outcome bias** | Evaluating decisions by outcome instead of process | Good decision + bad outcome = fine; don't change process |
| **Apophenia** | Seeing patterns in random data; evolutionary Type I error bias | 4-step validation: identify → hypothesize → backtest → accept/dismiss |
| **Gambler's fallacy** | "Black is due" after 8 reds | Probability has no memory; each independent event is independent |

### Deepened Treatments

| Bias | New Content |
|---|---|
| **Overconfidence** | Three types (overestimation, overplacement, miscalibration); miscalibration predicts worst trading performance; women outperform men by ~1% net |
| **Overtrading** | Brain regions activated by monetary gains overlap with cocaine; sports-car HF managers underperform minivan drivers by 3.22%/year; DSM-V gambling criteria adapted |
| **Anchoring** | Four specific manifestations: entry point, price high/low, P&L high, economist-prior anchoring |
| **Herding** | Five causal mechanisms; wisdom of crowds conditions; bubble trading framework (fit MA, exit on close below) |

### Turnaround Tuesday

After stocks fall Thursday+Friday then drop >5% on Monday:
- Average Tuesday return: **+4.3%** (since 1960)
- Compare: all days after 5% drop average only +0.9%
- Mechanism: weekend worry selling → Monday momentum shorts → Tuesday squeeze (nobody left to sell)

## 7. Risk Appetite Scoring (1–10 Scale)

| Score | Profile | Prescription |
|---|---|---|
| 1–4 | Risk averse | Set MINIMUM trade size; don't exit before triggers; use trade quotas |
| 5–7 | Moderate | When at HWM, define aggressive risk amount (e.g., up $6M → risk $1M aggressively) |
| 7.5–8 | **Optimal** | Natural balance of risk-taking and discipline |
| 8–9 | Strong | Needs strict rules: max daily/monthly/yearly loss, cooling-off levels |
| 10 | Sensation-seeking | Honestly evaluate: professional or gambler? |

### Three Effects That Shift Risk Appetite

1. **Zero-bound effect**: Trading more nervously as P&L approaches zero/negative
2. **End-of-period effect**: Less risk near year-end
3. **House money effect**: Reckless after big gains; dissipates over time

## 8. Adaptation Framework

### Three Variables to Track Daily

1. **Volatility** — where relative to recent history
2. **Liquidity** — good, bad, or ugly
3. **Trend/Range status** — trending, rangebound, or neither

### Regime Identification (Three Methods)

1. **20-day high/low test**: New 20-day high or low in last 10 days? Yes = trending
2. **Dual MA matrix**: Price vs. 100-hour and 200-hour alignment
3. **Eyeball**: Often a cursory glance reveals the regime

### Crisis Market Rules (VIX > 40)

1. Position size is the difference between winning and losing
2. Keep an open mind — don't assume "it's already moved enough"
3. Overbought/oversold indicators are useless in true crises
4. Have courage — get involved, don't hide

### Strategy Decay

- Profitable strategies slowly disappear as markets discover and crowd into them
- **Kill your darlings**: if P&L shows a strategy no longer works, cut it regardless of emotional attachment
- Example: Donnelly's lead/lag correlation trading worked 2003–2013, then was crowded out by algos and universal access to cross-asset data

## 10. Self-Management Systems

### Daily Sheet / Morning Prep

- Mood score (1–10)
- Opportunity score (1–10)
- Risk budget formula: (avg of mood + opportunity scores) / 10 × max risk
- Technical levels for the day
- Plan with specific trades, stops, targets
- Personal reminders
- **"Never schedule meetings before noon — that is your time to trade"**

### 12-12 Journaling System

Email a peer daily with:
- **12 hours back**: summary of what happened, what you did
- **12 hours forward**: plan with specific stops and risk levels

Forces accountability, habit formation, and real-time feedback.

### One Goal System

After journal review, set ONE short-term goal (few months max). Not 5–15 goals. Focus compounds.

### Default Mode Network (DMN) Awareness

- Identify your default mental pattern when idle
- Positive DMN: scanning charts, generating ideas, creative thinking
- Negative DMN: urge to press BUY/SELL, checking phone at 3am
- **Alpha traders hold two contradictory skills**: ability to sit and do nothing for long periods AND ability to act aggressively without hesitation

## 11. Variance and Process Diagnostics

### Variance vs. Bad Process (9-Item Diagnostic)

**Variance (acceptable):**
1. Was wrong a lot — being wrong is part of trading
2. Got unlucky — bad news, stopped out at highs

**NOT variance (fix immediately):**
3. Reading market well but losing money (process breakdown)
4. No plan
5. Overtrading
6. Left stale orders (poor attention)
7. Poor discipline (moved stops, doubled losers)
8. Market regime changed, haven't adapted
9. Manager pressure / existential fear / personal problems

### Call Option P&L Structure

Using YTD-adjusted risk scaling (Type II formula):
- Downside capped (monthly stop loss reduces risk when losing)
- Upside uncapped (risk increases as YTD P&L grows)
- Simulation: similar bad-year outcomes but dramatically better good-year outcomes

## 14. Method Transfer Notes

### What Alpha Trader Adds Beyond Art of Currency Trading

1. **Narrative analysis**: The 7-stage cycle and four pillars for narrative expertise provide the missing strategic timing layer — knowing not just *what* to trade but *when* the market is ready to trade it.

2. **Evolved conviction system**: Type I/II/III with the explicit formula (3% + 10% YTD) creates the call-option P&L structure mathematically. More practical than the fixed 1/3/6% from Art of Currency Trading.

3. **Self-assessment infrastructure**: Risk appetite scoring, decisiveness scoring, Daily Sheet with mood/opportunity scoring, 12-12 journaling, One Goal system — these are concrete tools for ongoing self-management, not just awareness of biases.

4. **ACH framework**: The 8-step hypothesis testing process from intelligence analysis is directly applicable to trade filtering — forces consideration of alternatives before commitment.

5. **Crisis trading rules**: Explicit framework for VIX >40 environments that overrides normal indicators and demands different position sizing.

6. **Strategy decay awareness**: The mechanism by which edges erode (crowding, technology democratization) and the discipline to kill beloved strategies when P&L proves they're dead.

7. **Variance diagnostics**: The 9-item checklist distinguishing variance from process failure prevents both (a) abandoning a sound methodology during a bad run and (b) persisting with a broken methodology while blaming luck.
