## Checklist 1: Five-Star Trade Qualification

Use this checklist to grade trade conviction before sizing. Each star maps to a branch of Donnelly's fusion framework.

| # | Branch | Question | Pass Criteria |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Fundamentals** | Is there recent information suggesting a change NOT fully reflected in price? | Delta vs. Level test: the change matters, not the absolute level |
| 2 | **Cross-market signals** | Do correlated variables (rates, commodities, equities) suggest currency is away from fair value? | ≥2 variables from the currency driver map confirming the direction |
| 3 | **Positioning** | Do sentiment and positioning support (or at least not hurt) the idea? | COT not extreme against you; sentiment/positioning divergence is a bonus |
| 4 | **Technicals** | Do multiple levels/patterns support? Is one or more of the Seven Deadly Setups in play? | Convergence of ≥2 technical indicators at entry zone |
| 5 | **Gut feel** | Do you feel confident? Or is something nagging despite the analytics? | Head and gut aligned = maximum conviction |
| 6 | **Catalyst** (optional) | Is there an upcoming event that could push the currency? | Donnelly: catalysts overrated; currencies move when they want |

**Scoring**:
- 3 stars (1% risk): ≥3 branches passing
- 4 stars (3% risk): ≥4 branches passing with strong alignment
- 5 stars (6% risk): All 5 branches strongly aligned, rare (~10/year)

**Crosswinds test**: If you find roughly equal bullish and bearish indicators across branches → do NOT trade. Pass.

## Checklist 2: FX Driver Regime Identification

Use at the start of each trading day/week to determine which analytical mode applies.

| # | Question | If YES → |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Is global risk aversion elevated (VIX spike, credit spreads widening, EM selloff)? | **Global regime**: Domestic fundamentals irrelevant. Trade safe havens (JPY, CHF, USD) vs. high-yielders. Dollar Smile State 1. |
| 2 | Is a single macro theme dominating headlines (China slowdown, oil crash, tariffs)? | **Thematic regime**: One variable drives everything. Identify the theme and trade the currencies most exposed. |
| 3 | Is the world broadly stable with differentiated country-level data? | **Domestic regime**: Focus on individual country monetary policy, data surprises, CB signals. Standard FX driver analysis applies. |
| 4 | Which of the four global drivers is dominant right now? | Rank: global growth, commodities, risk aversion, geopolitics. The dominant driver determines which correlations are most reliable. |
| 5 | Where are we on the Dollar Smile? | State 1 (recession/risk-off → USD strong), State 2 (muddling → USD weak → EM sweet spot), State 3 (US boom + hawkish Fed → USD strong) |

**Cross-reference**: Combine with Booth's 65% global factor assessment. If global factor is dominant, Willer's EM tactical signals may be overridden.

## Checklist 3: Correlation / Intermarket Divergence Trade

Use when scanning for lead/lag opportunities each morning.

| # | Step | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Scan overlays** | Review 50–60 overlay charts (each major pair vs. key drivers from the currency driver map). Hourly charts, 21–42 day lookback. |
| 2 | **Identify divergence** | Is a currency meaningfully out of line with its primary driver(s)? |
| 3 | **Confirm across drivers** | Check secondary and tertiary drivers for the same currency. Do ≥2 variables confirm the same direction? |
| 4 | **Validate the correlation** | (a) Does it make logical sense? (b) Is it likely to persist? (c) Rising AND falling prices in sample? (d) Third variable driving both? |
| 5 | **Assess momentum** | Which variable has more momentum — the driver or the currency? Only trade if the driver is leading and currency is lagging. |
| 6 | **Check for crosswinds** | Are there other drivers pointing the opposite direction? If yes → pass. |
| 7 | **Entry** | Enter in the direction suggested by the divergence. |
| 8 | **Stop loss** | Set firm stop at time of entry. Do NOT widen even if divergence increases (tempting trap). |
| 9 | **Target** | Where overlay suggests fair value convergence. |

**Key rule**: The larger the divergence, the more attractive the trade *appears* — this is a TRAP. Stick to predetermined stop regardless.

## Checklist 4: News / Data Event Trading

Use for any scheduled economic release or central bank event.

### Pre-Event

| # | Step | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Note the NewsPivot** | Record spot price immediately before the release. This is the reference for the rest of the session. |
| 2 | **Know expectations** | Consensus forecast AND whisper number (what market actually expects). Know the standard deviation of typical misses. |
| 3 | **Assess positioning** | Is the market heavily positioned one way? Extreme positioning amplifies moves in the forced-liquidation direction. |
| 4 | **Choose strategy** | Pre-commit to one of the three strategies based on your assessment of likely scenarios. |

### Post-Event Decision Tree

| Scenario | Strategy | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Extreme data (>2σ miss/beat) + market positioned wrong | **Go With** | Wait for first algo wave + brief consolidation; enter on the bounce in the direction of the surprise. Stop above/below NewsPivot. |
| Headline strong but details weak (or vice versa) | **Fade the Kneejerk** | Take the other side of the algo-driven move once you've verified headline/detail divergence. |
| Price reverses back through NewsPivot despite unambiguous news | **Bad News / Good Price** | Follow the price, not the news. Currency revealing underlying supply/demand. |
| Unexpected CB move (rate change, new policy) | **Go With — Rule #9** | Jump on immediately, aggressively. Do NOT fade. Only exit if price goes back through initial NewsPivot. |
| Unscheduled CB action | **Always Go With** | Same as above, even more aggressive. |

### Post-Event

| # | Step |
|---|---|
| 1 | Expect momentum to dwindle after 11:00AM NY as London goes home |
| 2 | If NewsPivot is breached in the wrong direction → exit or reverse |
| 3 | Log trade in journal with factor tag (DATA, CB, GEOPOLITICAL) |

## Checklist 5: CB Meeting EV Framework

Use before any central bank decision where you have a view differing from market pricing.

| # | Step | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Do the work** | Read every speech, statement, interview from the CB since last meeting. Track evolution of language. |
| 2 | **Determine market pricing** | What does the OIS / rate futures market price as probability of each outcome? |
| 3 | **Form your own view** | Assign probabilities to each outcome based on your reading of the CB communication. |
| 4 | **Construct EV grid** | For each outcome: (your probability) × (estimated pip move) = weighted payoff. Sum across outcomes. |
| 5 | **Check breakeven** | What probability would you need for the trade to be positive EV? If your assessed probability is comfortably above breakeven → trade. |
| 6 | **Size** | Use conviction framework (1/3/6%). CB trades rarely qualify as 5-star unless the mispricing is dramatic. |
| 7 | **Execute** | Position ahead of the decision. |
| 8 | **Post-decision** | If result matches your view → take profit as planned. If unexpected → Rule #9: go with the surprise, do not fade. |

**Cross-reference**: Combine with Willer's CB catalyst framework for EM-specific dynamics.

## Checklist 6: Seven Deadly Setups — Entry Decision Tree

Use when a potential setup is forming. Each setup has specific entry, stop, and target rules.

### Setup Identification

| # | Setup | Trigger Signal |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Slingshot Reversal** | Key S/R level breaks but fails to hold; price reverses back into old range |
| 2 | **Shooting Star / Hammer** | Hammer/shooting star candle forms at a price extreme after a sustained trend |
| 3 | **Deviation** | Spot >1% away from 100-hour MA |
| 4 | **Volume Spike at Extreme** | Large volume spike coinciding with a price extreme |
| 5 | **Broken Triangle** | Triangle breaks one direction, fails, reverses through the other side |
| 6 | **Double/Triple Top/Bottom** | Multiple tests of the same level, supported by fundamental view |
| 7 | **Sunday Gap** | Major weekend news → gap at Sunday open |

### Entry / Stop / Target Quick Reference

| Setup | Entry | Stop | Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Slingshot | 10bp inside recaptured level | 10bp beyond new extreme | 1.5–2× ADR or next major S/R |
| Hammer/Star | Close of the candle | Beyond the extreme wick | Based on macro view / next level |
| Deviation | At current level when >1% from 100hr MA | +0.7% beyond entry | Reversion to MA (or partial: sell some at +100 pips) |
| Volume Spike | After 2–3 bars (60–90 min) of declining volume | Beyond the price extreme | Top of the waterfall (pre-crash level) |
| Broken Triangle | When price closes back inside the triangle | Outside the broken side | Opposite side of triangle or beyond |
| Double/Triple Top | Near the repeated level | Just beyond the level (tight) | 1–2× the distance from entry to level below/above |
| Sunday Gap | Opening hour of Sunday trading | 1× ADR away | 90% of gap filled |

### Multi-Setup Convergence

Count how many setups are firing simultaneously. More = higher conviction:
- 1 setup = standard trade (3-star)
- 2 setups = strong trade (4-star candidate)
- 3+ setups = "triple play" (5-star candidate if fundamentals also aligned)

## Checklist 7: Position Sizing and Risk Management

Use for every trade entry.

| # | Step | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Calculate adjusted free capital** | Initial free capital + YTD P&L |
| 2 | **Determine monthly limits** | Stop loss = 10% of adjusted free capital; Target = 2× stop |
| 3 | **Check monthly P&L** | How much of monthly stop loss has been consumed? If near limit → reduce or stop. |
| 4 | **Grade conviction** | 3-star (1%), 4-star (3%), 5-star (6%) of adjusted free capital |
| 5 | **Identify stop loss level** | Combine: (a) technical convergence + 20% daily range buffer, (b) ADR-based (1.2× short-term, 2.5× medium-term) |
| 6 | **Calculate position size** | $ at risk / loss per unit if stop triggers. Include estimated slippage (2–3 pips G10, 20–30 pips EM). |
| 7 | **Vol-adjust** | Wider stop in higher-vol pair → smaller position. Different pairs, different notionals for same dollar risk. |
| 8 | **Check total portfolio risk** | All positions combined ≤ 8% of free capital. 3–5% is more reasonable most of the time. |
| 9 | **Write down stop loss** | Before or immediately after entry. Automate execution. |
| 10 | **Do NOT move stop** | Exceptions only: trailing to lock profit, reducing ahead of event risk. |

**Post-stop**: 4-hour cooling-off before re-entering same trade.

## Checklist 8: Behavioral Self-Assessment

Use daily (morning pre-market) and whenever you notice emotional intensity during trading.

### Morning Assessment

| # | Question | Red Flag | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | What is my emotional state? | Euphoric, frustrated, anxious, desperate to make back losses | Reduce planned position sizes by 50%. If severe → stay flat. |
| 2 | Am I holding a view I've been married to for too long? | Same directional bias for >2 weeks without reassessment | Force yourself to articulate the bear case. Get flat and reassess. |
| 3 | Am I looking forward to a specific trade too much? | FOMO; feeling like "this is the big one" | Rule #6: No mo' FOMO. There will always be another trade. |
| 4 | Am I trying to prove I'm smarter than the market? | Picking tops/bottoms; avoiding obvious trades because "too easy" | Goal is to make money, not be right. |
| 5 | Did I have a fight, get bad news, or sleep poorly? | Any external stressor | Reduce size or go flat. Trading is not therapy. |

### During-Session Triggers

| Signal | What It Means | Immediate Action |
|---|---|---|
| Counting P&L before bed / mid-session | Euphoria / overconfidence | Cut 30–50% of positions immediately (Cheer Hedge) |
| Feeling invincible, wanting to high-five | Winner's tilt — about to give it back | Get flat. Reset. |
| Angry at the market, asking "WTF?" | You are on the wrong side | The move probably has further to run. Reassess view. |
| Moving your stop "just a little bit" | Loss aversion + anchoring | Stop. Revert to original stop. If already moved → close position now. |
| Adding to a loser without plan change | Hope-based trading | Close the add immediately. Reassess the whole position. |
| Trading a pair you haven't watched for >2 months | Boredom / novelty-seeking | Close it. Rule: watch a market 1–2 months before trading it. |

### Slump Detection

| Indicator | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Consecutive losing days | ≥5 days → initiate slump protocol |
| Monthly P&L | Approaching monthly stop loss (>7% of adjusted free capital consumed) |
| Gain/loss ratio declining | 3+ consecutive months of deteriorating ratio |
| Random low-conviction trades increasing | More than 3 unplanned trades in a week |

**Slump protocol**: (1) Square all. (2) 20% size. (3) Talk. (4) Research. (5) Perspective. (6) Wait for Five-Star.

## Checklist 9: Daily Trading Routine

Donnelly's recommended daily process, adapted for the full em-macro-trader architecture.

### Pre-Market (Before 7AM NY)

| # | Step |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Emotional assessment** — Run Checklist 8 morning assessment |
| 2 | **Overnight review** — What happened in Asia/Europe? Any gaps, data, CB actions? |
| 3 | **Correlation scan** — Run Checklist 3 (scan overlays for divergences) |
| 4 | **Regime check** — Run Checklist 2 (global vs. domestic focus; Dollar Smile state) |
| 5 | **Calendar check** — What data releases today? Any CB speakers? Apply Checklist 4 pre-event steps for each. |
| 6 | **Technical levels** — Mark key S/R, where MAs are, whether any Seven Deadly Setups are forming |
| 7 | **Write the plan** — On paper or Post-It: today's directional bias, target trades, risk limits |

### Active Trading (7AM–12PM NY)

| # | Step |
|---|---|
| 1 | Execute plan from pre-market prep |
| 2 | For any new trade: Run Checklist 1 (Five-Star qualification) → Checklist 7 (sizing) |
| 3 | For any data event: Run Checklist 4 (news trading) |
| 4 | Monitor for behavioral red flags (Checklist 8 during-session triggers) |

### Post-Overlap (12PM–5PM NY)

| # | Step |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Protect gains** — Donnelly gave back 20–40% of profits in the afternoon. Keep hands off the keys. |
| 2 | **Research mode** — Read, analyze, prepare for next session |
| 3 | **Journal** — Log all trades with entry/exit, conviction, factor tags, and post-trade notes |
| 4 | **P&L OHLC** — Record Open, High, Low, Close of today's P&L. Update MTD/YTD trackers. |

### Weekend

| # | Step |
|---|---|
| 1 | Go home flat every Friday. Reduces gap risk + gives mind rest. |
| 2 | Review weekly performance: win%, gain/loss ratio, conviction accuracy |
| 3 | Update longer-term views (macro regime, positioning shifts) |
